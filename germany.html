Germany

Neuschwanstein Castle

    Neuschwanstein Castle is a fairytale-like castle located in the Bavarian Alps near the town of Füssen
    Commissioned by King Ludwig II of Bavaria in the 19th century, Neuschwanstein Castle is renowned for its picturesque setting, stunning architecture, and romantic ambiance
    Often referred to as the "castle of the fairy-tale king," Neuschwanstein Castle inspired the design of Disney's Sleeping Beauty Castle and is one of the most visited castles in Europe

Brandenburg Gate, Berlin

    The Brandenburg Gate is an iconic monument and symbol of Berlin and Germany's unity and resilience
    Built in the late 18th century, the Brandenburg Gate is located in the heart of Berlin and served as a symbol of division during the Cold War, standing in the no-man's land between East and West Berlin
    Today, the Brandenburg Gate is a symbol of peace and unity, attracting visitors who come to admire its neoclassical architecture and historical significance

Cologne Cathedral (Kölner Dom)

    Cologne Cathedral is a stunning Gothic cathedral located in the city of Cologne on the banks of the Rhine River
    Construction of the cathedral began in the 13th century and continued for over 600 years, resulting in one of the largest and most impressive cathedrals in Europe
    A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Cologne Cathedral is known for its intricate façade, towering spires, and magnificent interior, including the Shrine of the Three Kings
