Mount Fuji

    Mount Fuji, or Fuji-san, is Japan's highest peak and an iconic symbol of the country.
    Located on Honshu Island, Mount Fuji stands at 3,776 meters (12,389 feet) tall and is an active stratovolcano.
    Mount Fuji is revered as a sacred mountain in Japanese culture and has been the subject of countless artworks, poems, and pilgrimages throughout history. It is also a popular destination for hikers and climbers, attracting thousands of visitors each year to ascend its slopes and admire its majestic beauty.

Tokyo

    Tokyo is the capital city of Japan and one of the world's most populous metropolitan areas, known for its modern skyline, vibrant street life, and eclectic mix of traditional and contemporary culture.
    As the political, economic, and cultural center of Japan, Tokyo offers endless attractions and experiences for visitors, including iconic landmarks like the Tokyo Tower, Senso-ji Temple, Meiji Shrine, and the bustling neighborhoods of Shibuya and Shinjuku.
    Tokyo is also renowned for its world-class dining, shopping, entertainment, and technological innovations, making it a must-visit destination for travelers from around the globe.

Kyoto

    Kyoto is a historic city located in the Kansai region of Japan, known for its well-preserved traditional architecture, stunning gardens, and numerous temples and shrines.
    As the former imperial capital of Japan for over a thousand years, Kyoto is home to some of the country's most significant cultural and historical landmarks.
    Key attractions in Kyoto include the Fushimi Inari Taisha Shrine, Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion), Kiyomizu-dera Temple, and the historic Gion district known for its traditional tea houses and geisha culture.
