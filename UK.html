Big Ben and the Houses of Parliament, London

    Big Ben is the nickname for the Great Bell of the clock at the north end of the Palace of Westminster in London.
    The Houses of Parliament, also known as the Palace of Westminster, is the seat of the UK Parliament and an iconic symbol of British politics.
    Together, Big Ben and the Houses of Parliament are among the most recognizable landmarks in the world, attracting millions of visitors each year.

Tower of London

    The Tower of London is a historic castle located on the north bank of the River Thames in central London.
    Built by William the Conqueror in the 11th century, the Tower of London has served variously as a royal palace, fortress, and prison throughout its history.
    Today, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to the Crown Jewels, attracting visitors who come to explore its medieval architecture and learn about its rich history.

Buckingham Palace, London

    Buckingham Palace is the official residence of the British monarch in London and the administrative headquarters of the monarchy.
    Built in the 18th century, Buckingham Palace is renowned for its neoclassical architecture, grand State Rooms, and iconic balcony where the royal family appears on special occasions.
    The Changing of the Guard ceremony, held outside Buckingham Palace, is a popular tourist attraction and a quintessential British experience.
