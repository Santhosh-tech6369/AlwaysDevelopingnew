Brazil

Christ the Redeemer (Cristo Redentor), Rio de Janeiro

    Christ the Redeemer is an iconic statue of Jesus Christ located atop the Corcovado mountain in Rio de Janeiro
    Standing at 30 meters (98 feet) tall, with arms outstretched, the statue is one of the most recognizable symbols of Brazil and Christianity.
    Completed in 1931, Christ the Redeemer offers panoramic views of Rio de Janeiro and the surrounding landscape, attracting millions of visitors each year to marvel at its beauty and significance.

Iguazu Falls (Cataratas do Iguaçu)

    Iguazu Falls is a spectacular natural wonder located on the border between Brazil and Argentina.
    Comprising a series of 275 waterfalls spread over nearly 3 kilometers (1.86 miles), Iguazu Falls is one of the largest and most impressive waterfall systems in the world.
    Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Iguazu Falls is surrounded by lush rainforest teeming with biodiversity, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Amazon Rainforest (Amazônia)

    The Amazon Rainforest is the world's largest tropical rainforest, covering much of northwestern Brazil and extending into several other South American countries.
    Known for its unparalleled biodiversity, the Amazon Rainforest is home to millions of species of plants, animals, and insects, many of which are found nowhere else on Earth.
    Visitors to the Amazon can explore its dense jungles, winding rivers, and diverse ecosystems, experiencing firsthand the wonders of this ecological treasure and learning about the importance of rainforest conservation.
